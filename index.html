MEETING QUARTERS

Wrocław, Poland
Co-authors: Agata Proniak, Piotr Pańczyk

  1. Exhibited
    in the Museum of Architecture

Architectural and urban concept for church as a center of a newly created district in Wrocław - Nowe Żerniki. It is made of six tallest modules, which have an expressively bent roof in the furthest, south-west corner. It forms an interesting belfry, perfectly visible from the whole districts axis. Right behind the church there is a public square as a central, public space.

TOUCH THE BARN

Vidzeme, Latvia
Co-author: Piotr Pańczyk

  1. Participant
    in Stone Barn Meditation Camp competition

  2. Shortlisted
    on Bee Breeders website

Monumental stone barn adapted and revitalised for the purpose of a meditation center in Latvia. There are many ways to meditate. But could it be possible to meditate through architecture? Touch the Barn project explores this theme by proposing a lightweight steel structure and series of accessible ramps warping around existing historical building.
